Most weight-loss prescriptions are one-and-done — a dose, a refill, and the patient is on their own. Aeonic runs a feedback loop instead: we start low, watch how their body actually responds, and tune the dose to that response. It's the difference between guessing and measuring.

Excess weight isn’t cosmetic — it’s the dial on their healthspan.

Visceral fat drives insulin resistance, inflammation, and cardiovascular risk that compound silently for years. Losing it the right way — and keeping it off — is one of the highest-leverage things they can do for how long, and how well, they live.

The cost of waiting

Risk compounds quietly

Every year at an elevated set point raises their odds of type-2 diabetes, fatty liver, and heart disease. The earlier they intervene, the more they protect.

The detail behind their protocol.

GLP-1 receptor agonists mimic a gut hormone that slows gastric emptying and signals fullness to the brain — so the patient eats less without fighting constant hunger, while insulin sensitivity and glucose control improve in parallel.
Starting low and stepping the dose up on a clinician-set schedule lets their body adapt, minimizes GI side effects, and finds the lowest effective dose that holds their results — rather than overshooting.
