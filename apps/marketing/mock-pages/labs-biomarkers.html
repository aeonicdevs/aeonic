Labs & biomarkers

The biology, measured.

Every protocol starts and ends with data. Aeonic reads across systems — not a single number, but the interacting markers that explain how a patient actually feels and functions — then builds the plan around them and re-tests to prove it worked.

What we measure

Six systems, read together.

A protocol is only as good as the picture behind it. These are the panels that map a patient’s physiology — and where every plan begins.

Metabolic

Glucose & insulin

Fasting glucose, insulin, HbA1c, and metabolic markers — the engine of weight, energy, and long-term risk.

Hormonal

The endocrine axis

Sex hormones, thyroid, cortisol, and the HPA-T-G axis that sets baseline energy, mood, libido, and recovery.

Inflammation

Immune & inflammatory

hs-CRP, homocysteine, and immune markers — the low-grade inflammation that quietly accelerates aging.

Cardiovascular

Lipids & heart

Advanced lipids, ApoB, and cardiovascular markers — beyond standard cholesterol to real risk.

Cellular

Micronutrient & methylation

Vitamin D, B-vitamins, minerals, and methylation status — the cofactors everything else runs on.

Composition

Body & performance

Body composition, lean mass, and functional markers tracked over time — outcomes you can feel and measure.

How we read them

Optimal, not just “normal”.

A result inside the reference range isn’t the same as a result that’s right. Aeonic interprets labs three ways most care doesn’t.

Optimal ranges

Beyond the reference range

“Normal” is a population average that includes a lot of unwell people. We target the optimal range for performance and longevity — where patients actually feel their best.

In context

Markers interact

No value is read alone. Hormones, metabolism, and inflammation move together — we interpret the system, not the single number.

Over time

Trended, not snapshotted

One panel is a data point; a trend is the truth. We track markers across re-tests to see what the protocol is actually doing.

Labs in the loop

Data in, protocol out, re-tested.

Labs aren’t a one-time gate — they’re the feedback loop that keeps every protocol honest.

01 · Baseline

Establish the starting point

Comprehensive panels capture where the patient actually is across every system — the foundation the plan is built on.

02 · Build

Aim the protocol at targets

Therapies and dosing are selected toward specific marker targets — so progress is objective, not a guess.

03 · Re-test

See what moved

Repeat panels show what changed — the proof the protocol is working, in the data, not just the feeling.

04 · Adjust

Tune to the response

The protocol evolves to the result and the loop runs again — optimization that compounds over time.

In the app

Labs that make sense.

Every result is auto-digitized and trended in the patient’s portal, highlighted by the protocol it relates to — with a plain-language clinician summary on every panel, so the data is legible, not intimidating.
